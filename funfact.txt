SJDM: The Rising City.
SJDM is the most populous city in Central Luzon.
CSJDM is known as the 'Balcony of the Metropolis' due to its elevated topography.
Mount Balagbag is a popular destination for hikers and mountain bikers.
The city is home to several major resettlement projects in the Philippines.
San Jose del Monte officially became a city on September 10, 2000, making it the first city in Bulacan.
Originally composed of only six barangays, CSJDM now has 62 barangays, divided into two districts.
The La Mesa Eco Park, though often associated with Quezon City, the CSJDM covers the 33 hectares of lush forest and recreational space.
Our Lady of Lourdes Grotto Shrine is famous pilgrimage site, especially during Holy Week, attracting thousands of devotees.
The town was established on March 2, 1752, making it historically significant in Bulacan’s development.
The city is named after Saint Joseph, the patron saint of workers and families.
CSJDM is only 42 kilometers from Manila, making it a gateway city between Metro Manila and Bulacan.
The city’s motto is “Arya San Joseño!”, a rallying cry for progress and unity.
CSJDM is sometimes called the “Gateway to Bulacan” because of its proximity to Metro Manila.
It hosts SM City San Jose del Monte, one of the largest malls in the area, serving as a hub for shopping and leisure.
The city is known for its People’s Park & Sports Complex, a venue for community events and recreation.
CSJDM will be the northern terminal of the MRT-7 line, connecting the city directly to Quezon City and North Avenue in Metro Manila. 
The city celebrates Tanglawan Festival. “Tanglawan” means torch, symbolizing enlightenment and guidance.
Saint Joseph Worker Parish Work began in 1842 and was completed in 1854, giving the church its classic 19th-century architecture.
The Obrero Festival to honor the working class of the society. San Jose Ang Manggagawa, San Isidro Ang Magsasaka at San Pedro Ang Mangingisda.
Quirino Highway is the primary link between SJDM and Northern Quezon City.
Muzon is considered the central transport hub of the city, connecting District 1 and 2.
The Indakan Festival showcases street dancing and youth creativity.
The Sapang Palay Resettlement Project made the city a major relocation site in the 1960s.
Project MONTE was developed to bridge the gap in SJDM's paratransit data mapping.
MONTE stands for Multimodal Optimization and Navigation for Transport Efficiency.
The project utilizes QGIS and OpenStreetMap to digitize previously unmapped jeepney routes.
Developed by CSanSci students, MONTE aims to improve the commuting experience in the city.
The app features real-time traffic analysis specifically calibrated for the city’s peak hours.
MONTE’s routing engine is designed to prioritize local TODA terminals for tricycle commuters.
